## 1. Irish Government Grants

### 1.1 Enterprise Ireland

#### Pre-Seed Start Fund (PSSF) -- BEST EARLY FIT

- **Amount:** EUR 50,000 or EUR 100,000 via Convertible Loan Note (CLN)
- **Structure:** EUR 100K provided in two tranches of EUR 50K each. CLN converts to equity at 20% discount on a qualifying round of EUR 250K+. 3% interest rate, 5-year term.
- **Includes:** 10 mentoring sessions + Enterprise Ireland Development Adviser
- **Eligibility:** Must have an MVP or be live in beta with early customer validation. Must be in manufacturing or internationally traded services sectors. Must demonstrate a validated customer proposition.
- **Fit for 8gent Jr:** STRONG. You have a working app, a clear user need, and the "internationally traded services" angle works since AAC is a global need. The MVP requirement is already met.
- **URL:** https://www.enterprise-ireland.com/en/supports/pre-seed-start-fund
- **Action:** Contact Enterprise Ireland directly for current application windows.

#### HPSU Feasibility Study Grant

- **Amount:** Up to EUR 30,000 (covers 70% of eligible expenditure)
- **Purpose:** Investigate viability of a new export-oriented business or proposition
- **Eligibility:** New start-up or early-stage company exploring a genuinely innovative, export-focused product/service with protectable IP
- **Fit for 8gent Jr:** MODERATE. Could fund market research, SLP consultations, and user research across EU markets. The "export-oriented" requirement aligns well since AAC is needed globally.
- **URL:** https://www.enterprise-ireland.com/en/supports/hpsu-feasibility-study-grant

#### Innovative HPSU Fund

- **Amount:** Up to EUR 1.2 million in funding; up to EUR 800,000 in co-funded equity investment
- **Eligibility:** Must demonstrate potential for EUR 1M in sales and 10 jobs within 3 years. Must have an innovative product for international markets with protectable IP.
- **Fit for 8gent Jr:** LOW at current stage. The "EUR 1M sales / 10 jobs in 3 years" bar is very high for a free product. This becomes relevant only if you build a commercial model alongside the free AAC (e.g., institutional licensing, white-label for schools/HSE).
- **URL:** https://www.enterprise-ireland.com/en/innovative-hpsu-fund

#### Innovation Voucher

- **Amount:** EUR 10,000 (fully funded) or EUR 20,000 (co-funded, 50% match)
- **Purpose:** Explore a business opportunity or problem with a registered knowledge provider (university, institute of technology, etc.)
- **Eligibility:** Irish-registered limited company, fewer than 250 employees, under EUR 50M turnover. Rolling applications year-round, ~4 week evaluation.
- **Fit for 8gent Jr:** STRONG. Use this to partner with a university research group (e.g., Trinity's ADAPT Centre, UCD, or TU Dublin) on AI speech synthesis for AAC, or on user research with autistic children. Costs nothing but the application time.
- **URL:** https://www.enterprise-ireland.com/en/supports/innovation-voucher
- **Action:** Apply now. No deadline -- rolling applications.

#### Competitive Start Fund / Exploring Innovation Grant

- **Amount:** Historically EUR 50,000 equity investment (10% stake)
- **Status:** The Competitive Start Fund has been replaced/rebranded. Enterprise Ireland now offers the **Exploring Innovation Grant** and the **Pre-Seed Start Fund** as successors.
- **URL:** https://www.enterprise-ireland.com/en/supports/exploring-innovation-grant
- **Action:** Check current EI website for active calls.

### 1.2 Local Enterprise Offices (LEOs)

#### Feasibility Study Grant

- **Amount:** Up to EUR 15,000 (50% of costs in S&E Region; 60% in BMW Region)
- **Covers:** Market research, consultancy, technical development/prototyping, salary/own labour research
- **Eligibility:** Individual or business exploring manufacturing or internationally traded service ventures. Max 10 employees. Must be in LEO's geographic area.
- **Fit for 8gent Jr:** STRONG. Low barrier, fast turnaround. Use it to fund speech therapist consultations, user research, and market validation.
- **URL:** https://www.localenterprise.ie/discover-business-supports/financial-supports/feasibility-grant/
- **Action:** Contact your local LEO (based on your registered address in Ireland).

#### Priming Grant

- **Amount:** Up to EUR 80,000 (50% of investment). Can go to EUR 150,000 in exceptional cases for businesses with potential to graduate to Enterprise Ireland.
- **Covers:** Capital items, salary costs, consultancy/innovation/marketing, general overheads
- **Eligibility:** Micro-enterprise within first 18 months of start-up. Max 10 employees. Max EUR 15,000 per full-time job created.
- **Fit for 8gent Jr:** STRONG. If you register a company and are within 18 months, this can fund your full-time development salary. EUR 80K covers roughly 12 months of solo dev work.
- **URL:** https://www.localenterprise.ie/discover-business-supports/financial-supports/priming-grant/
- **Action:** Register as a company first, then apply through your local LEO.

### 2.4 European Accessibility Act (EAA)

#### Implications and Opportunities

- **Effective:** June 2025
- **What it does:** Sets EU-wide accessibility requirements for products and services. Creates legal obligation for accessible digital products.
- **Opportunity for 8gent Jr:**
  - The EAA creates **demand** for accessible technology solutions
  - Companies need to comply, creating a market for accessibility consulting and tools
  - EU may offer grants or tax benefits for companies exceeding compliance
  - AI-powered assistive tech (like AAC) is specifically cited as a growth area
- **Strategic angle:** Position 8gent Jr not just as a charity project but as infrastructure that helps institutions comply with the EAA. Schools, hospitals, and public services all need accessible communication tools.
- **URL:** https://commission.europa.eu/strategy-and-policy/policies/justice-and-fundamental-rights/disability/union-equality-strategy-rights-persons-disabilities-2021-2030/european-accessibility-act_en

## 3. Crowdfunding Platforms Comparison

### 3.1 GoFundMe

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Platform fee** | 0% (free to create) |
| **Transaction fee** | ~2.9% + EUR 0.25 per donation (varies by country) |
| **Donor tips** | Optional voluntary tip to GoFundMe from donors |
| **Model** | Donation-based (keep what you raise, no all-or-nothing) |
| **Best for** | Personal stories, medical/disability causes, community support |
| **Strengths** | Largest personal fundraising platform. Strong emotional/story-driven campaigns perform well. No minimum goal required. Immediate access to funds. |
| **Weaknesses** | No reward structure. Perceived as "charity" not "product." Hard to build recurring community. Tax treatment is ambiguous (donation vs income). |
| **Disability track record** | STRONG. GoFundMe is the dominant platform for disability-related fundraising. Families regularly raise for AAC devices, therapy, and assistive tech. |
| **URL** | https://www.gofundme.com |

### 3.2 Kickstarter

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Platform fee** | 5% of funds raised (only if successful) |
| **Payment processing** | 3-5% additional |
| **Total effective fee** | ~8-10% of funds raised |
| **Model** | All-or-nothing (must hit goal to receive funds) |
| **Best for** | Product launches, creative projects, hardware, apps with tangible deliverables |
| **Strengths** | Large audience of backers looking for innovative products. Reward tiers create engagement. All-or-nothing model creates urgency. Media coverage for successful campaigns. |
| **Weaknesses** | All-or-nothing risk (lose everything if you miss goal). Reward fulfillment can be expensive and time-consuming. 8-10% total fees. Must deliver rewards or face reputational damage. |
| **Disability track record** | MODERATE. Open Bionics (3D-printed bionic hands) is a notable success story. Assistive tech is underrepresented but campaigns that do launch tend to get strong community support. |
| **Hidden costs** | Budget 15-20% total deductions (fees + dropped pledges + taxes + shipping) |
| **URL** | https://www.kickstarter.com |

### 3.3 Open Collective

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Host fee** | 10% of incoming funds (via Open Source Collective) |
| **Payment processing** | Additional Stripe/PayPal fees |
| **Total effective fee** | ~13-15% |
| **Model** | Ongoing/recurring donations with full financial transparency |
| **Best for** | Open-source projects, community-driven development, transparent budgeting |
| **Strengths** | Full financial transparency (all expenses visible publicly). Recurring donation model. Strong open-source community. Fiscal hosting (no need to set up your own nonprofit). No reward fulfillment burden. |
| **Weaknesses** | Highest fees of the three. Smaller audience than GoFundMe/Kickstarter. Requires ongoing community engagement. Less emotional storytelling surface. |
| **Open source fit** | EXCELLENT. If 8gent Jr is open source, Open Collective is the natural home. 2,500+ open source projects already use it. |
| **URL** | https://opencollective.com |

### 3.4 Recommendation

**Use a multi-platform strategy:**

1. **GoFundMe** for the emotional campaign launch. Lead with James + Nick's story. Target EUR 10-25K from the general public. This is your awareness driver.

2. **Open Collective** for ongoing open-source sustainability. This is where developers and the AAC community contribute monthly. Target EUR 500-2,000/month recurring.

3. **Skip Kickstarter** unless you have a hardware component (e.g., a dedicated AAC tablet). The all-or-nothing model is risky for a solo founder, and the reward fulfillment overhead is a distraction.

### 3.5 What Works Best for Disability/Accessibility Tech Campaigns

- **Story-driven beats reward-driven.** Disability crowdfunding campaigns succeed on emotional connection, not perks. A 2-minute video of Nick using the app is worth more than any reward tier.
- **Community amplification.** Autism/disability communities on social media are tight-knit and supportive. One viral share from a parent or SLP can 10x a campaign.
- **Transparency.** Show exactly where money goes. Open Collective's transparent ledger is powerful for this.
- **Updates.** Regular development updates keep donors engaged and drive recurring giving.

## 4. GitHub Sponsors

### 4.1 Setup Requirements

1. Have a GitHub account with 2FA enabled
2. Join GitHub Sponsors program
3. Complete sponsored developer profile
4. Create sponsorship tiers (up to 10 monthly + 10 one-time tiers)
5. Submit bank information (Stripe Connect)
6. Submit tax information (W-8BEN for non-US individuals)
7. Enable your profile for sponsorship

**URL:** https://docs.github.com/en/sponsors/receiving-sponsorships-through-github-sponsors/setting-up-github-sponsors-for-your-personal-account

### 4.2 Fee Structure

- **Personal account sponsors:** 0% fee -- 100% goes to you
- **Organisation sponsors:** Up to 6% fee
- **Payment processing:** Handled by GitHub (no additional Stripe fees to you)
- **This is the best deal of any platform.**

### 4.3 Tax Implications for Irish Residents

- GitHub Sponsors income is **taxable income** in Ireland
- Treated as self-employment income (sole trader / Schedule D Case I or II)
- Must register with Revenue as self-employed if not already
- File annual income tax return (Form 11)
- Subject to:
  - **Income Tax:** 20% (standard) / 40% (higher rate above EUR ~42,000)
  - **USC:** 0.5-8% depending on income
  - **PRSI Class S:** 4.2% (from Oct 2025) with EUR 650 minimum
- Deductible expenses: hosting costs, equipment, software, home office proportion
- GitHub cannot provide tax advice; consult a tax professional
- Keep records for 6 years

**Important:** GitHub complies with Irish tax reporting requirements. You will need to provide tax identification details.

### The Story

My name is James Spalding. I'm a software engineer and a single father in Ireland. My son Nick is 8 years old. He's autistic and non-verbal.

Every day, Nick has thoughts, feelings, preferences, and ideas that he cannot express through speech. Like hundreds of thousands of children worldwide, Nick relies on AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication) to be heard.

Here's what I discovered when I went looking for help:

- Commercial AAC apps cost EUR 200-400. Per year. For a child who needs it to say "I'm hungry."
- Most AAC tools are designed by engineers, not by parents who live this every day.
- The best symbol libraries (like ARASAAC) are free and open -- but the apps that use them are not.
- Children's data is often harvested by the very apps meant to help them communicate.

So I built one. **8gent Jr** is a free AAC app that will never charge families, never harvest children's data, and never disappear behind a paywall.

---

### What 8gent Jr Does

- **Symbol-based communication** using ARASAAC's open pictogram library (13,000+ symbols, CC-BY-NC-SA licensed)
- **AI-powered voice synthesis** so every child can have a voice that sounds like a child, not a robot
- **Adaptive learning** that grows with the child's vocabulary and communication patterns
- **Education integration** with curriculum-aligned content for Irish schools
- **Privacy-first architecture** with zero data harvesting, zero tracking, zero ads
- **Works offline** because communication doesn't stop when WiFi does
- **Open source** because no child's voice should be proprietary
